Spare Parts Modeling Assumptions

🔧 Repairable items

Behavior: A unit may fail multiple times during the period. Failed units are repaired and returned to service.
Model: Poisson — failures may recur and are not limited to one per unit.
Examples: Pump, motor, gearbox, valve, compressor.

🔁 Consumable items (replace on failure)

Behavior: Item is not repaired. Each failure consumes a new spare.
Model: Poisson — expected demand may exceed the installed base.
Examples: Fuse, filter, bearing, mechanical seal, belt.

⚠️ Non-repairable (single failure per unit)

Behavior: Each unit can fail at most once during the planning horizon.
Model: Binomial — maximum required spares ≤ fleet size.
Examples: One-time cartridge, rupture disk, safety pin, single-use device.

📈 Normal model (approximation)

Behavior: Mathematical approximation when expected demand is large.
When valid: Expected demand is sufficiently high and variability is moderate.

⛔ End-of-life / wear-out

Behavior: Reliability approaches zero within the planning horizon.
Model: Deterministic — no uncertainty remaining; all units are expected to fail.

Degradation Modeling Assumptions

📏 Linear degradation

Model: D(t) = a + b·t + ε
When to use: Stable wear, corrosion, or erosion with no acceleration.
Examples: Uniform wall thinning, steady abrasion, predictable wear.

📈 Exponential degradation

Model: D(t) = a·exp(b·t) + ε
When to use: Chemical aging, corrosion acceleration, fatigue damage.
Examples: Corrosion under insulation, oxidation, creep damage.

📐 Power-law degradation

Model: D(t) = a·tᵇ + ε
When to use: Physics-based growth laws or crack propagation trends.
Note: ReliaSoft often rejects this if RUL becomes unrealistically large.

🌊 Wiener process (stochastic degradation)

Model: D(t) = μ·t + σ·W(t)
Strengths: ISO 13381 compliant, preferred ReliaSoft model, provides reliability & confidence bounds.
Examples: Corrosion, wear, fatigue damage, thickness loss.

✅ Model selection guidance

  • Use Wiener when variability and uncertainty matter
  • Use Linear for conservative engineering estimates
  • Use Exponential for accelerating degradation
  • Avoid Power-law if RUL becomes unrealistically large

Final choice: Lowest AIC/BIC + physical realism.
